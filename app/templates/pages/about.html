eBooks: An Overview

An eBook, short for "electronic book," is a digital version of a traditional print book that can be read on electronic devices such as eReaders, tablets, smartphones, and computers. eBooks offer the convenience of portable reading, allowing users to carry and access multiple titles from a single device. They come in various formats, including EPUB, PDF, and MOBI, each optimized for different devices and reading experiences.

One of the key benefits of eBooks is their accessibility. They can be downloaded instantly from online retailers or libraries, making it easy to acquire new reading material without leaving home. Additionally, eBooks often feature adjustable text sizes, customizable fonts, and built-in dictionaries, enhancing the reading experience for users with different preferences and needs.

With the growth of digital technology, eBooks have become increasingly popular among readers and publishers alike, offering a flexible and eco-friendly alternative to traditional print books.
